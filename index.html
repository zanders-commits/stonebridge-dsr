§01  ·  Problem & Solution

From four disconnected systems to one connected platform

The Problem

Stonebridge's operations teams manage purchasing, invoice processing, inventory, and recipe costing across separate platforms — each with its own login, its own reporting, and no shared view of spend.

Purchasing happens through supplier logins and phone orders. Invoices flow through a separate AP tool before they reach accounting. Inventory and culinary tracking live in yet another system, rolled out to only a handful of properties so far.

The result: no single place to see what's being spent, no way to price-shop across suppliers in one basket, and manual work at every handoff between systems.

The Answer

Reeco brings purchasing, AP automation, inventory, and culinary management into one connected platform — with a direct, real-time integration into M3 as the system of record for accounting and budget data.

One Login One Checkout, Every Supplier One Automatic Reconciliation

"One of the major reasons we went away from iBuy is because you guys clearly have a much bigger, better functioning, real-time checkbook. So if I want to be able to run those checkbook reports to say — hey, you're over in guest supplies already for the month — I can."

§02  ·  The Platform

Everything back-of-house runs on, in one place

Centralized Marketplace

See every supplier and every item price side by side in one basket, instead of checking each supplier's site separately. One checkout, even when the order splits across multiple suppliers behind the scenes.

AI-Assisted Ordering

The platform learns ordering history and par levels over time and suggests a cart for review, so operators aren't rebuilding the same order from memory every week.

Budget & GL Visibility At The PO Point

See active spend against monthly budget before an order is placed, with optional approval gates when a department is trending over.

Purchasing & Receiving

Mobile receiving with item-level confirmation, and a human-in-the-loop support team for damaged or short shipments — not just a form to fill out.

AP Automation With Three-Way Match

Invoices match automatically to POs and receiving confirmations, then sync directly to M3. No manual invoice coding once the system learns your patterns.

Inventory & Culinary

Storeroom-based counts, bar inventory by the bottle, and recipe-level food costing that updates as supplier prices change — down to the individual dish.

§07  ·  Frequently Asked Questions

The questions we hear most

How long does it take to go live?

AP automation alone is typically live within about two weeks of signing — connecting to M3, mapping GL codes, and importing vendors happens in week one, with corporate and property-level training completing go-live in week two.

Purchasing is sequenced inside that same four-week rollout, going live in week four: supplier onboarding and credential setup happen alongside AP, then a validation call, then Purchasing training and go-live. Inventory typically layers in around three weeks after core go-live; Culinary is chef-led and follows the same pattern. Multi-property rollouts move in phased cohorts rather than all at once, so operations teams aren't managing a big-bang cutover.

What does the transition off our current AP tool look like?

Reeco's setup team handles vendor outreach directly — collecting your supplier list and managing the process of redirecting invoice delivery to Reeco, rather than leaving that legwork to property teams.

Is Reeco's data secure?

Yes. Reeco is SOC 2 Type II compliant.

Does Reeco replace our GPO relationship?

No — Reeco is a GPO partner, not a replacement. Approved-item enforcement and purchasing guardrails can be configured to reflect your GPO agreement inside the platform.

Does Reeco support barcode scanning?

Yes — barcode scanning for physical inventory counts is live today in the mobile app, and something we can pull up and show you directly during the pilot. Barcode scanning on the receiving side, matching items as they arrive against a PO, is still in development.

Can Reeco flag savings from hitting supplier free-shipping thresholds?

This is a capability we're actively tracking for Stonebridge specifically — using order data to flag when consolidating orders across departments would hit a supplier's free-delivery or case-size minimum. It's logged with our product team but isn't part of the initial pilot scope, so we'll keep you posted as it develops.
